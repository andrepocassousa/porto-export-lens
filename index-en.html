Background

The problem this project solves

Port Wine exporters make market decisions based on historical experience and commercial intuition. The data exists — the IVDP publishes detailed export statistics by destination country, volume and value since 2006 — but it is rarely processed systematically. A typical KAM cannot answer in 30 seconds: "which market has grown the most in value per litre over the past 5 years?"

Methodology

Data pipeline

1
Source
Export files by country published by the IVDP — historical series 2006 to 2025, with volume (litres), value (€) and average price per litre.
2
Cleaning
Country name normalisation, removal of aggregate totals, handling of missing values. Final dataset: 2,461 clean records in CSV.
3
Analysis
SQL queries in DuckDB to calculate rankings, YoY changes, and segmentation by market profile (volume, growth, price).
4
Visualisation
Interactive dashboard with Chart.js. Code available on GitHub for reproducibility and methodology audit.

Strategic interpretation

What the data reveals

Four findings with direct implications for an export strategy — framed from the perspective of a KAM who uses data to prioritise markets and prepare conversations with importers.

Priority opportunity
South Korea — premium and accelerating
South Korea has grown over 200% in value since 2019 and maintains an average price of €9.66/litre in 2025 — the third highest of all markets. It is the only country appearing simultaneously in the growth top and price top. For an export KAM, this is the most interesting market profile: still room to grow, but already valuing the product.
€9.66/litre +18% in 2025 +200% since 2019
Priority opportunity
UAE — the world's most expensive market
At €13.38/litre, the UAE is the market where Port Wine commands the highest price per litre — and growing 38% in 2025. The combination of high price, strong growth, and luxury consumer profile makes this market strategic for exporters looking to elevate their portfolio positioning. Distribution via Dubai provides access to the entire Middle East.
€13.38/litre +38% in 2025 €2.29M in 2025
Market to watch
Mexico and Finland — strong growth from a low base
Mexico (+57%) and Finland (+48%) are the fastest-growing markets in 2025, but both start from a relatively small base (€1–1.3M). The pattern is consistent with the early stage of market penetration — exactly the moment when an exporter can establish position before the competition. Finland has state distribution (Alko), which simplifies entry but requires specific certifications.
Mexico +57% Finland +48% Small base, high momentum
Warning signal
USA — premium but contracting
The US offers the highest price per litre among major markets (€9.55/l) and remains third largest in absolute value (€37.8M). However, it shows a contraction trend since 2019 — falling from first to third place in value. Over-reliance on traditional European markets (France, UK, Netherlands) at lower price points suggests a volume strategy that may be undermining the category's global positioning.
€9.55/litre -1.2% vs 2024 Declining since 2019
Limitations and next steps

What this study does not cover

Data scope
The analysis covers bottled Port Wine only. Bulk and special categories (Tawny, LBV, Vintage) — representing roughly half of total IVDP exports — are not included. A complete analysis would require combining all three categories.
Market share
Without total import data by country (e.g. UN Comtrade), it is not possible to calculate Portugal's market share in each destination. Knowing that South Korea imports a large amount of wine overall but little Port would be an additional relevant insight.
Competitive context
IVDP data reflects the sector in aggregate, not the performance of individual exporters. It is not possible to distinguish category growth from the growth of a specific player.
Next steps
Integrate UN Comtrade API to calculate Portuguese market share by country. Add special category analysis (Tawny, LBV). Build a volume forecasting model with Facebook Prophet for the top 10 markets.
